I am Kin Lane, the API Evangelist...

I took the summer off last year for a project we called Drone Recovery. I began investing in, and flying drones as part of what we were doing, and unexpectedly got interested in the world of drones. Once I returned I began studying the intersection of drones and my career as the API Evangelist, better understanding how companies are using APIs on the drone, on mobile devices, and in the cloud.

I have a client who expressed interest in my thoughts about an API system for drone post-production, and API driven market and ecosystem. I'm looking for projects to help me push forward my storytelling around APIs, so I set out to craft a prototype API to flush out my thoughts around the idea, acting like I'm developing an API, but just do it as an exercise, without all the cost.

This is my Drone API prototype. It is a Github driven prototype where I walk through all the steps I would take to design, develop, manage, and evolve a drone API. I'm looking to establish an evolving, but simple overview of the API, but with all the detail of how I got here right below the surface, so anyone could learn from my story.

Drones Are Interesting

I did not like drones at first. Because of military drones, and Internet of Things hype I just wasn't interested, but over the last year I've come to find them very intriguing. They are the poster child for Internet connected devices, offering the best and worst of what happens when you connect devices to the Internet.

Like much of the API sector, drones fascinate me, but not enough to actually jump into the business, developing devices, tools, APIs, and offering services in the sector. This is what makes my API prototype process great, is that I can explore my ideas, understand the possibilities, and tell stories without any of the financial risk and headach ethat comes along with doing startups.

This prototype is meant to look and act like a Drone API, if I was operating it, has much of the detail about getting started, documentat, code, the APIs, as well as much of the other aspects of operating an API. The entire project is available here on the home page, but to get much of the story I recommend taking a look at the road map, change log, and the blogs, as it tells much of the story of how I explored this idea, with the resulting website as the current snapshot of my drone API prototype.

API Status & Performance

The Legal Department

Here is where the legal aspect of things kicks in, with the details from the lawyers regarding how all of this will work.

Terms of Service

The legal terms of service that guide the platform, setting the tone for the conversation between platform operaters, partners, and the consumers who will be building applications using these API resources.

Privacy Policy

This is the legal details covering the privacy of platform operators, partners, developers, and end-users. It should cover all the aspects of data storage, collection, security, and anything that would impact the privacy of users.

Service Level Agreement

The legal document that covers the level of service that is expected as part of the API contract. Setting the details for service availability, reliability, security, and many of the common areas that could negatively impact business operations.

Deprecation Policy

The contract made with partners and consumers regarding how long an API will be around, setting expectations for how long a company can depend on an API, and what the standard procedure is for shutting one down.

Licensing

Thinking through the licensing of the different layers of this API operation from the API, schema, to the SDK code, everything needs a license. We emphasize the open licensing of the API, schema, data, and code generated as part of this API platform operations.

API Operations Guide

We started by documenting our API design process and adding to a guide that we shared with everyone involved. Now we add in details from every other area of API operation including deployment, management, testing, monitoring, and other areas of our operations.

The guide provides a standardized approach to how we handle our API operatioins from definition to deprecation. It has guidance for a variety of API design implementations including REST, gRPC, GraphQL, and Hypermedia APIs. Providing a rich set of approaches for development teams to follow.

We are constantly updating the guide, keeping it in tune with any new approaches to how any team is doing APIs. It is up to each team to contribute their best practices, helping keep the API operations guide up to date, and services all stops along the API lifecycle.

There is a PDF version, as well as a Github repository for managing the guide, which we regularly publish and share as PDF or even print copies with different internal and partner groups. The guide helps standardize how we approach the design and operation of our keeping things more consistent, and interoperable. We share our operations guide publicly to help encourage our partners and wider community to adopt similar best practices, as well as sharing their own.

These prototypes are part of a new type of storytelling I'm exploring when it comes to helping folks understand the best practices emerging in the API space.
